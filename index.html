Simplifying Scheduling For Managers: Redesigning Homebase

Unifying Request Management And Cross-Platform Consistency For Manager Task Efficiency


Jan 2025 - Mar 2025
Ethan Cheng
Role - UX Researcher | UI Designer
Collaborators - Sophie Chin | Ryen Huang | Jacob McCoy

Through our user research, I found that pain points of using Homebase were a lack of sorting and visiblity for viewing employees' scheduling requests and desired hours, and only being able to approve requests through the app but not the website.

Working with 3 other UX designers, I redesigned the existing Homebase app to improve its readability, ease of use, and to consolidate managers' workflows into one platform as a course assignment in UC San Diego. I contributed to redesigning the Homebase platform to improve readability and usability while consolidating the managers' workflows into a single, consistent experience across devices. Our solution improved the interface of the weekly schedule view and the accessibility of using organizing functions, creating a more efficient scheduling process for busy managers.

UX Flow
Identifying a managers's UX flow, under the persona of James Ray, I found the entry point was a calendar-based dashboard which I wanted to keep for familiarity.

Quick Schedule
Changing the shift creation to a pop-up on the calendar dashboard allows managers to create shifts quickly and seamlessly, while cutting down on excessive navigation and letting managers keep information about availability on screen in the background.

Two variations were created, with the second ultimately being favored by user testing, due to a simpler appearance and the ability to message through the same pop-up panel.
